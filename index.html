✦ Inside the Pages

What These Books Are About

Not fancy poetry. Just real feelings, put into words.

Heavy with Light

This book came out of college years — the kind of time when you feel everything but don't know where to put it. It's about love that didn't work out, friends who mattered a lot, a father who left a mark, and the slow work of finding yourself.

The poems don't try to sound smart. They just try to be honest. Some are short. Some are long. Some don't even rhyme. But each one came from something that actually happened.

❤️ Love & Longing 👨 Father & Family 🤝 Friendship 💔 Grief 🌱 Growing Up 🕊️ Letting Go

Where It Hurts to Feel

The second book goes a bit deeper. It's for the feelings you sit with alone — the ones you don't talk about at dinner or mention in conversation. It's about the ache that stays even after things get better.

If the first book was about going through things, this one is about understanding them — the slow, uncomfortable way. Written for anyone who has ever felt too much at once and had nobody to tell.

🌑 Inner Pain 🪞 Self-Reflection 💜 Silent Emotions 🌧️ Healing 🔥 Burning Out ✨ Hope

Everything You Left Behind

The third book is about what remains — the things that don't leave even when people do. The memories that stay longer than they should. The versions of yourself you outgrew but still think about.

This one is quieter than the first two. Less about the loudness of pain and more about the silence that follows. Written for anyone who has ever looked back and felt the weight of everything they've left behind.

🍂 Letting Go 🕰️ Memory 💚 Moving On 🌿 Acceptance 🪟 Looking Back ✨ Peace
✦ The Writer

About the Author

P
P Bharath Chowdary
#PBCPoetry · BCA Student · Ballari, Karnataka

A computer science student who started writing poetry because there were things he couldn't say any other way. Not a professional poet. Not trained in literature. Just someone who felt a lot and figured that writing it down was better than keeping it all inside.

Three books now — each one written from a different place, a different phase. The first from the middle of it. The second from the aftermath. The third from everything that was quietly left behind along the way.

"Poetry doesn't need to be understood by everyone — it just needs to be felt by someone."
